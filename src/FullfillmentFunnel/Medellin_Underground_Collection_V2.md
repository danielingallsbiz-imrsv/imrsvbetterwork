# IMRSV Fulfillment Funnel: Medellín Underground Collection V2

This is a strictly curated list of underground, local-heavy, and exclusive spots in Medellín. It intentionally excludes all hostels (e.g., Black Sheep, Yellow House), tourist-heavy areas, and repeats from the previous list, drawing heavily from local Spanish-speaking forums and deep-cut Reddit recommendations.

## 🍸 Hidden Bars & Speakeasies (New Additions)
1. **La Cruda**
   - *What it is:* From the outside, it looks like a dirty, rundown wooden door. Inside, it's a vibrant, neon-lit modern cocktail bar.
   - *Why chosen:* The ultimate "don't judge a book by its cover" speakeasy experience, catering less to tourists and more to a young, trendy local crowd.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @lacrudamedellin]
2. **Mombasa**
   - *What it is:* A clandestine bar hidden inside a restaurant in El Poblado.
   - *Why chosen:* Requires a daily-changing password to enter (obtained from a waiter, a hidden message in the restaurant menu, or their website). Very exclusive, intimate afro-house and jazz vibe.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @mombasa.ba]
3. **SicosiS Bar**
   - *What it is:* An edgy, grunge-style hidden bar.
   - *Why chosen:* Plays niche metal/grunge music serving high-end cocktails. It naturally filters out the mainstream reggae/pop crowd, keeping it strictly for alternative locals.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @sicosismetallounge]
4. **Resistencia**
   - *What it is:* A hidden rock/alternative bar in the center.
   - *Why chosen:* A staple of the local alt-scene, far removed from the expat bubbles.
   - *Link:* N/A (Local knowledge required)

## 🍽️ Highly Exclusive & Clandestine Dining
5. **Mi Palenque (Comuna 13)**
   - *What it is:* A tiny, very localized spot focusing purely on authentic Afro-Colombian and Chocoan coastal food (sudado con coco, authentic paella).
   - *Why chosen:* It skips the standard "gringo" burger/pizza spots for hyper-regional, culturally rich cuisine.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @mipalenquec13]
6. **Gabriela Cocina Experimental**
   - *What it is:* An experimental, chef-driven Colombian restaurant.
   - *Why chosen:* Under the radar compared to giants like El Cielo, offering an incredible, intimate tasting menu experience without the massive tourist waitlists.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @gabrielacocinaexperimental]
7. **Clandestino (Cinematic Dinners)**
   - *What it is:* An underground dining experience heavily tied to cinema.
   - *Why chosen:* They project a film and serve a multi-course dinner inspired directly by the movie. Extremely niche, perfect for high-end dates.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @clandestinomed]
8. **Idílico**
   - *What it is:* A highly exclusive, small-menu restaurant.
   - *Why chosen:* The menu rotates constantly based on seasonal local ingredients. It's the "chef's choice" spot for foodies avoiding the flashy Provenza strip.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @idilico.restaurante]
9. **Bao Bar Singapore (El Retiro)**
   - *What it is:* Authentic Singaporean/Malay cuisine.
   - *Why chosen:* Located outside the city in El Retiro (a town locals escape to). It's a closely guarded secret among expats and wealthy locals who want deeply authentic Asian flavors.
   - *Link:* [Instagram @baobarsingapore]

## 💻 Elite Local Workspaces (Strictly NO Hostels)
*(Because public cafes get crowded, locals use distinct coworking cafes or hidden mall spaces)*
10. **Tinkko Laureles**
    - *What it is:* A serious, corporate-leaning coworking space.
    - *Why chosen:* Not a hostel, not a noisy cafe. It is built for absolute silence, fast fiber internet, and focused work.
    - *Link:* [Website](https://tinkko.com/)
11. **Nómadas (El Tesoro Mall)**
    - *What it is:* Hidden, free coworking zones built into the high-end El Tesoro shopping center.
    - *Why chosen:* A local hack. It's safe, the internet is stable, you have access to incredible food downstairs, and it avoids the "digital nomad cafe" tax.
    - *Link:* [El Tesoro Info](https://eltesoro.com.co/)
12. **La Casa Redonda**
    - *What it is:* A creative-focused coworking space.
    - *Why chosen:* Highly tailored to the local design/arts crowd rather than transient tourists. Very relaxed but professional.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @lacasaredondacoworking]
13. **Rituales Cafe (Laureles)**
    - *What it is:* A highly rated, purely local coffee shop.
    - *Why chosen:* They win awards for their coffee, but it remains a quiet, neighborhood staple rather than a tourist destination. Great place to get work done.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @ritualescafe]

## 🎸 The Real Rock & Punk Underground
14. **Rock Symphony (Torres de Bomboná)**
    - *What it is:* A classic, 20+ year old rock bar.
    - *Why chosen:* The Bomboná towers in downtown are the nucleus of real Medellín alternative culture. This is as authentic as it gets.
    - *Link:* N/A (Located in Pasaje Comercial Bomboná)
15. **The Cave**
    - *What it is:* A deeply underground rock spot near the university in the city center.
    - *Why chosen:* Basement vibes, cheap drinks, entirely local student/punk crowd. 
    - *Link:* N/A 
16. **House of Demons**
    - *What it is:* A heavy metal and hard rock bar in Laureles (towards LA 33).
    - *Why chosen:* Unapologetically heavy. A stark contrast to the reggaeton clubs of Poblado.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @houseofdemonsbar]
17. **Donde Chepe 1 (Parque El Poblado)**
    - *What it is:* The original rock offshoot of a popular bar.
    - *Why chosen:* You have to specify "Chepe 1" (rock) vs "Chepe 2" (reggaeton). It’s a gritty, loud, local favorite right on the park.
    - *Link:* N/A
18. **Berlin Bar (Calle 10)**
    - *What it is:* An alternative bar with billiards.
    - *Why chosen:* A long-standing staple for the alt-crowd to drink heavily, play pool, and listen to rock/punk.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @berlinbarmedellin]

## 🎭 Deep-Cut Local Experiences
19. **Proceso Cultural El Hormiguero**
    - *What it is:* A local punk/cultural collective.
    - *Why chosen:* They organize the actual grassroots underground punk shows and cultural events in the city. Following them is the key to the real underground scene.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @procesoculturalelhormiguero]
20. **Eating at La Minorista (The Market)**
    - *What it is:* The massive, chaotic farmers market of the city. Specifically, eating at "Aquí Paró Lucho".
    - *Why chosen:* Rough around the edges, but this is exactly how the working-class locals eat. Incredible, massive portions of authentic food.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @aquiparolucho]
21. **Chocoan Food in El Centro**
    - *What it is:* Finding the tiny, unmarked restaurants serving food from the Pacific coast (Chocó).
    - *Why chosen:* Completely off the tourist radar. It’s a different culinary world (heavy coconut, unique fish dishes) right in the chaotic city center.
    - *Link:* N/A (Requires exploring the center near the minorista).
22. **San Felix Paragliding & Vista al Vuelo**
    - *What it is:* High up in the mountains above the city.
    - *Why chosen:* Paragliding is known, but going specifically at night to the "Vista al Vuelo" restaurant/bar area gives you a freezing-cold, stunning local party vibe overlooking the entire valley.
    - *Link:* [Instagram @vistaalvuelo]
23. **Río Claro Ecological Reserve (Speleology)**
    - *What it is:* A river reserve 3 hours away.
    - *Why chosen:* Skipping the crowded Guatapé for deep-cave exploration (speleology) and canopying over marble riverbeds.
    - *Link:* [Website](https://www.rioclaroreservanatural.com/)
